How Often Do I Need To Get a Tire Replacement?

Tires are easy to ignore when they’re working correctly, but they’re still an essential part of your vehicle. Every driver in Dallas or Fort Worth, Texas, counts on their wheels to take them where they need to go. As you might expect, tires withstand incredible amounts of pressure and friction in the process.

Eventually, however, these forces will take their toll on your tires. When that happens, even tire repair won’t be enough to get them back in shape — instead, you’ll need to start thinking about tire replacement. Here’s our guide to figuring out when it’s time to replace your tires and knowing what to look for in a new set.

Know When To Replace Your Tires

The lifespan of any set of tires will vary depending on factors such as their brand and how frequently they’re used. Despite this, you can safely estimate that your tires will last somewhere between three and five years — if you make tire maintenance a priority, that is. This timeline also assumes you drive between 12,000 and 15,000 miles annually. If you drive more than that in an average year, you might need a new set of tires sooner than expected.

One of the primary factors behind a tire’s lifespan is its tread depth. Tires with no more than 2/32” of tread are considered “bald” and cannot be used safely. For a quick way to gauge your tread depth, try the “penny test” — take a penny and stick it into your tire’s grooves with the head facing down. When the tread does not cover the top of Lincoln’s head, it’s time to look for new tires.

How Many Tires Should You Replace?

Let’s assume your car has a single worn-out tire and three others still in decent condition. In that case, you’d only need to replace the worn tire, right? Not necessarily. In some situations like these, you may still have to replace two or even all four tires.

This is because tires are designed to work alongside other wheels; your tires should be nearly identical for maximum performance. Knowing your tires have the same manufacturer, tread pattern, and speed rating is the key to getting the best results out of these components.

If you do opt to mix and match tires, be sure to follow some basic guidelines. First, ensure the tires on each of your car’s axles share a brand and tread type. You’ll also want to put older tires on the front axle when you can — installing newer tires at the rear of your vehicle will give you enhanced traction.

Don’t Skimp on Tire Quality

Tire savings shouldn’t come at the expense of tire quality. After all, your tires will be responsible for your safety (and your passengers’) on the road for the next few years. Because of that, it’s never truly in your best interest to cut costs by buying subpar tires when it’s time for tire replacement.

That said, finding a tire shop that offers excellent value for your money is definitely a good idea. As a first step, look for Dallas-area shops that provide online coupons and other opportunities to save on new tires.

Keep Tire Warranties in Mind

Even the best new tires can fail unexpectedly, but that shouldn’t deter you from investing in these vital components. Instead, try to find tires that deliver added peace of mind by offering a robust warranty.

You’re not alone if you’re wondering what makes for a good tire warranty.. The next time you visit your local Dallas-Fort Worth tire shop, feel free to ask the people working there about factors like:

  • Warranty coverage length
  • Guarantees regarding materials and workmanship
  • Maintenance guarantees
  • Geographical warranty coverage
  • Coverage of road hazards

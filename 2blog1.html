Dallas Oil Change Service: How it Impacts Vehicle Performance

Old oil can reduce your vehicle’s engine performance. By receiving regular preventative maintenance, such as changing the engine oil, you can ensure that your vehicle runs smoothly throughout your trip. Most drivers know that routine oil change service is a necessity. But how exactly does it impact vehicle performance?

In this article, we answer that question by breaking down the role that engine oil plays and explaining exactly how it contributes to the optimal performance and longevity of your vehicle.

Why Oil Change Service is Critical to Your Car’s Lifespan

If an engine is the heart of your vehicle, routine oil changes can be considered its lifeline. If you want to extend the lifespan of your vehicle and keep it operating at its best, oil change service is an essential task that should not be overlooked.

The oil performs several vital functions. Here are three major ways an oil change service can revitalize your engine and, ultimately, enhance your vehicle’s performance:

1. It Helps Circulate Clean Engine Oil

After an oil change, motor oil will begin to flow through your engine the moment you start the car. As it travels through your engine, clean oil can pick up deposits and prevent sludge from forming. The cleaner your engine is, the better it will run. This is why having clean motor oil is essential for keeping the engine properly protected and running smoothly.

2. It Lubricates Essential Engine Components

Once oil enters your vehicle’s engine, it adds a slippery, protective layer that works to reduce friction and protects these parts from corrosion. Lack of lubrication can result in increased friction and potentially costly damage to the engine. Regular oil changes also help maintain the optimal viscosity of the oil, ensuring that it can flow easily through the engine and reach all the necessary components.

3. It Prevents Engine Wear And Tear

Friction can occur when your engine continuously comes in contact with other components in your vehicle. If this does occur, it can produce excess heat in your engine, which generally results in wear and tear. Regular oil changes significantly help prevent such damage. Fresh, clean oil keeps the engine properly protected by reducing the impact of friction and preventing metal-on-metal contact. This helps avoid premature engine failure and costly repairs.
